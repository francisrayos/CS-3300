Which Streaming Music Services Are Best for Artists?

Francis Rayos del Sol (fmr32) | Sam Robiner (smr276) | Rosie Ross (rwr238)

Music streaming services provide users with a quick, easy, and typically cheap way to listen to millions of songs. But are these services as beneficial to artists? Do the most popular services among listeners give musicians fair pay? And how is this different for signed vs. independent artists? We found the answers to these questions by comparing the following popular streaming services: Apple Music, Deezer, Google Play, Napster, Spotify, and Tidal.

The chart to the right displays how independent and signed artists split their money with the streaming service and their label. Independent artists earn a larger piece of their raw profits, but they lose out on the resources and connections a label might provide them with.

If I replay my fav artist's song ten thousand times will they notice?

This chart shows the earnings of an artist if they get 10,000 plays on a service. If you're a Napster user you might be able to get your fav a few hundred dollars.

How popular is each service?

This chart shows how many paid and free users each service has. Now it's clear how artists can still make a profit on a service like Spotify - which pays an artist so little for each song play.

So how popular does an artist have to be to earn minimum wage?

The music staff shows the difference between an independent and a signed artists' necessary popularity for each streaming service. The minimum wage used is $1,472 a month, which is the equivalent of working full time for $9.20 an hour. The top of the chart shows the number of song plays necessary to reach minimum wage while the bottom shows what percentage of the service's users would have to play the artists music. Note how for Spotify the number of plays required is very high, but the % of their users that would have to listen is fairly low.
